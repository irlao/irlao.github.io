Pastel Cat Doll Pattern

You will need:

  1. One small skein of white yarn
  2. One small skein of black yarn
  3. Thread of black embroidery yarn
  4. Polyester stuffing
  5. One pair of safety eyes
  6. Your favourite crochet hook
  7. Your favourite sewing needle

Abbreviations:

  • sc = stitch
  • sc = stitch
  • dec = decrease
  • hdc = half double crochet stitch
  • dc = double crochet stitch
  • sl = slip stitch
  • blo = back loop only
  • flo = front loop only
  • fo = finish off
  • ( … ) = repeat whatever is in between the brackets
  • [ .. ] = total amount of stitches in that round

For the cats in the pictures I personally used 4.5 mm eyes and a 8.0 mm hook. I also used Paintbox Yarns Cotton DK yarn in the following colours:

  • Tan: 401 Sand Dune
  • Grey: 402 Cloud Grey
  • Cream: 403 Buttercream

Pattern:

  1. 6 in a magic circle [6] This will be the top side of the head
  2. ( inc ) x 6 [12]
  3. ( sc, inc) x 6 [18]
  4. ( 2 sc, inc) x 6 [24]
  5. ( 3 sc, inc) x 6 [30]
  6. ( 4 sc, inc) x 6 [36]
  7. sc around [36]
  8. ( 4 sc, dec) x 6 [30]
  9. ( 3 sc, dec) x 6 [24]
  10. ( 2 sc, dec) x 6 [18]
  11. ( sc, dec) x 6 [12]
  12. ( dec ) x 6 [6]

Finish off and shut. Leave a long thread.

I hope you’ve enjoyed crocheting this project! This pattern will always be available for free in this blog post. Make sure you check out our other free patterns as we post new and seasonal ones regularly.

The History of Amigurumi

Some historians think that the origin of this art can be found in China, where there are records of crocheted and knitted dolls from the Shang dynasty. The craft started to take hold in Japan with the interaction between China and Japan during the Edo period, at the start of the 17th century. Dive into the history here!
